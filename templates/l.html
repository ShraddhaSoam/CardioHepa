Heart

In recent years, India has witnessed a concerning rise in heart diseases, posing a significant public health challenge. Several factors contribute to this surge, including lifestyle changes, urbanization, dietary shifts, and increased stress levels. Liver

Several factors contribute in increasing liver diseases , including changing lifestyles, increased consumption of alcohol, unhealthy dietary habits, viral hepatitis infections (particularly hepatitis B and C), and a growing prevalence of non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD) due to obesity and diabetes. Limited access to healthcare services and diagnostics in rural areas exacerbates the problem, leading to underdiagnosis and delayed treatment.
